Frequently Asked Questions

Are there requirements
following adoption?

Yes! Most shelters require adopted animals to be spayed or neutered, within a period of time, regular veterinary care and be properly licensed. Be sure your pet always wears a collar with an ID tag. Provide nutritious food and fresh water for your pet. Make time for exercise, training, and play.

Aren't ALL shelter animals
just “secondhand” pets?

Many shelter animals are younger and will be starting their lives as companions. Many of the older pets had previous owners. However, most come housetrained. Just because they lived with someone else doesn't mean they wouldn't make a wonderful companion for you!

Is it difficult to adopt
from a shelter?

Every shelter has its own adoption policies. Their first priority is the pet and most require responsible adopters prepared for a lifelong commitment. An important part of the adoption process is to match the lifestyle and needs of the adopter with the personality and behavior of the pet.
